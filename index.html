Research Vision

From Self-Driving
to Self-Evolving

Our system goes beyond automation. It is a self-evolving laboratory that learns from each experimental cycle — continuously building intuition about 2D quantum systems.

By integrating large language models, physics-informed AI, and robotic instrumentation, the platform autonomously navigates the vast chemical and structural space of 2D quantum materials.

Past experimental outcomes directly inform future exploration strategies, creating an ever-improving loop of discovery.

Prof. Sanfeng Wu
Principal Investigator
Department of Physics  ·  Princeton University  ·  Wu Lab ↗

Sanfeng Wu is an assistant professor of physics at Princeton University. He did his undergraduate work at the University of Science and Technology in Hefei, China, and received his Ph.D. from the department of physics at the University of Washington in 2016. He was the Pappalardo Fellow in Physics at MIT before receiving his appointment at Princeton. His main research areas are condensed matter physics, two-dimensional quantum matter and devices, and quantum information processes. He has received several awards and honors, including the Moore Foundation Award for the EPiQS Flexible Funding Ideas (2023), an AFOSR Young Investigator Award (2023), and a Sloan Research Fellowship (2023). He is also an associated faculty member at the Princeton Materials Institute and the Princeton Quantum Initiative.
